Did you know that space is not completely empty? It contains a very low-density, but ever-present, form of energy and matter known as "dark matter" and "dark energy." Dark matter cannot be seen or detected directly, but its existence is inferred from its gravitational effects on visible matter in the universe. Dark energy, on the other hand, is believed to be responsible for the accelerating expansion of the universe. Together, dark matter and dark energy make up a significant portion of the universe's composition, even though they remain mysterious and largely unexplained by current scientific understanding.

Cosmic inflation is a theory that suggests the universe underwent an exponential expansion during the first few moments after the Big Bang. This rapid expansion, occurring within a fraction of a second, helps to explain various observed phenomena in the universe's large-scale structure. Proposed by physicist Alan Guth in the early 1980s, the theory addresses issues like the uniformity of the cosmic microwave background radiation and the large-scale structure of the cosmos.
